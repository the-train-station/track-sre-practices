# Build Your SRE Reading List

## Objective

Curate a personalized SRE reading list from the [Awesome SRE](https://github.com/dastergon/awesome-sre) repository. By the end of this exercise, you will have a prioritized, categorized reading list tailored to your role and growth areas.

## Step 2: Selection Criteria

Use these criteria to evaluate each resource. Rate each resource 1-5 on these dimensions:

| Criterion | Description | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Relevance** | Directly applies to my current role or next-quarter goals | High |
| **Credibility** | Written by practitioners with production experience | High |
| **Actionability** | Provides concrete techniques I can apply immediately | Medium |
| **Depth** | Goes beyond surface-level introduction | Medium |
| **Recency** | Published or updated within last 3 years | Low |

**Minimum threshold:** A resource should score at least 3/5 on Relevance AND Credibility to make your list.

---

## Step 3: Browse and Select

Open the [Awesome SRE repository](https://github.com/dastergon/awesome-sre) and browse sections matching your focus areas. For each resource you select, fill in one row of the tracking table below.

**Guidelines:**

- Aim for 15-25 resources total across all categories
- Include a mix of formats (articles, talks, papers, tools)
- Include at least 2-3 foundational/classic pieces
- Include at least 2-3 recent/cutting-edge pieces
- Do not add everything; curation means deliberate exclusion

---

## Step 4: Categorization Framework

Organize your selections into these tiers:

### Tier 1: Must Read (complete within 2 weeks)

Resources directly addressing your most urgent gap. Limit to 3-5 items.

### Tier 2: Important (complete within 2 months)

Strong resources for your focus areas. 5-10 items.

### Tier 3: Reference (bookmark for later)

Valuable but not immediately needed. Consult when a specific problem arises. 5-10 items.

## Step 7: Reflection Questions

After completing your Tier 1 reading, answer these:

1. What is the single most important thing you learned that you can apply this week?

2. Which resource surprised you most? Why?

3. Did any resource change your understanding of a concept you thought you knew?

4. What gap remains that you need to find additional resources for?

5. Which resource would you recommend to a teammate? Why?

---

## Tips for Effective Reading

- **Take notes while reading.** Write a one-sentence summary for each resource.
- **Discuss with peers.** Schedule a 30-minute chat after completing Tier 1.
- **Apply immediately.** For each article, identify one action for your current work.
- **Revisit your list monthly.** Your priorities will shift as you learn and as your systems change.
- **Share back.** If you find a great resource not in Awesome SRE, submit a PR.
